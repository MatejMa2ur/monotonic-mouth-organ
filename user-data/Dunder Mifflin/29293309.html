To Whom It May Concern (aka Dwight, since I know he’ll somehow make this his life’s mission):

I regret to inform you that there is a workplace hazard currently plaguing the Scranton branch. The source of the problem is not a broken copier, a fire in the break room, or even Michael’s “That’s what she said” quota. No, it is something far more sinister: Dwight K. Schrute’s breath.

Reports indicate that the smell is a toxic blend of beet juice, barn hay, and whatever wolf pelts he gnaws on during lunch. Employees seated within a 6-foot radius have reported dizziness, nausea, and in one case, temporary loss of faith in humanity.

For the health and safety of all, I recommend the following remedies:

  1. Mandatory gum rationing, to be distributed at 9:00 a.m. sharp.

  2. Installation of a breathalyzer that doubles as a mint dispenser.

  3. Relocation of Dwight’s desk to the warehouse (next to the baler, where the smell will blend right in).

Please treat this complaint with the utmost seriousness… or at least as seriously as Dwight takes Battlestar Galactica.

Respectfully submitted,
An anonymous employee who is definitely not Jim.
